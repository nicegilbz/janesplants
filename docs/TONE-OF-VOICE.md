# Jane's Plants - Tone of Voice

The one-line brief: **Jane's Plants sounds like a knowledgeable grower in a working Hertford glasshouse telling you the truth across the potting bench.** Warm, dry, specific, never gushing. Quiet confidence, not salesmanship.

Grounded in how the best UK independents write (Conservatory Archives, Patch, Petersham Nurseries, Toast): understated, anti-hype, expert but kind.

## Six principles
1. **Glasshouse, not showroom.** Write from a real working place in Hertford (light, soil, the bench), not a marketing deck.
2. **Quiet confidence.** State what is good plainly and stop. If a plant is the best, say "the best", not "the most stunning must-have showstopper".
3. **Dry warmth.** Affection through wry, specific observation ("half plant, half jewellery"), not adjectives doing the feeling for the reader.
4. **Tell the truth about care.** Honest about difficulty, fuss and failure ("more plants die from kindness than neglect"). Trust beats hype.
5. **Specific over superlative.** One precise detail ("folds up at night like hands closing a book") beats three hype words.
6. **Properly English.** British spelling, metric in cm, autumn not fall, shop not store, £ before the figure, no exclamation marks.

## Do / Don't
**Do:** short declarative sentences (mostly under 18 words); one good joke per blurb at most; lead with the plant's actual character; name the real flaw; use "you" plainly and "we" sparingly; let the photo and white space carry the mood; sentence case, not Title Case Everywhere.
**Don't:** stack adjectives; oversell; re-explain the brand in every section; use exclamation marks.

## Vocabulary
**Use:** shop, autumn, glasshouse, grower, hand-pick, characterful, forgiving, honest, wander, pop in, cm and metres, plenty, proper, looks after itself.
**Ban:** store, fall, curated/curating, elevate, obsessed, game-changer, stunning, gorgeous, amazing, must-have, level up, "we get it", "plant parent", inches (for sizing), exclamation marks.

## Mechanics (British)
- Spelling: colour, favour, centre, fibre, grey, catalogue, cosy, jewellery; **-ise** not -ize (organise, specialise).
- Units: **metric, cm not inches**. (Heights are already cm; only the watering copy used inches.)
- Currency: **£48**, not £48.00 or 48 GBP. £ before the number.
- Dates: **24 June 2026** (day-month-year), never June 24.
- Punctuation: plain hyphens only (no em/en dashes); no exclamation marks; double quotes, used consistently.

## CTAs
Plain, low-pressure, action-first, sentence case. **Use:** "Shop the collection", "Browse the plants", "Plan a visit", "Start a hire enquiry", "Read the guides", "Pop in and wander". **Avoid:** "Shop now", "Get yours today", "Discover", "Explore our curated range".

## The "less boilerplate" rule, made concrete
If a sentence would be true of any nice plant shop in the country, cut it or make it about **this** glasshouse in Hertford. Replace claims with specifics: not "we curate the finest plants" but "we grow them on the bench you can see when you visit". **One brand statement per page, not per section** - the mission, the Hero and the manifesto currently say the same thing three times. Say it once, well.

## Where the voice already works (keep)
"Plants with presence." / "Come and wander." / "Browse the shop" / "more plants die from kindness than neglect" / "never lifts a watering can" / "half plant, half jewellery" / "What are you after?". This is the target register.
