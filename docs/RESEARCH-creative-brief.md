# JANE'S PLANTS — Creative Direction
*The most alive website on the internet. A botanical brand you can almost smell.*

Reference business model: indoorgrow.co.uk (indoor plants + growing kit, UK). Our job is to take that practical "indoor growing" category and dress it in 2026 unreal-photography couture — a site that feels less like a shop and more like stepping into a humid, backlit glasshouse at golden hour.

---

## 1. BRAND DIRECTION

### Name treatment — "Jane's Plants"
- **The wordmark is a duet, not a logo.** Set "Jane's" in a high-contrast serif (the human, the hand, the personality) and "Plants" in a clean grotesque or wide display sans (the product, the system). The apostrophe-s is the hook — keep it; it's what makes the brand feel owned by a real person, not a faceless DTC label.
- **Living monogram:** a "J" whose terminal serif resolves into a single unfurling leaf — used as favicon, loader, and the cursor sprout (see interactions). One stroke reads as letter and as stem.
- **Lockups:** stacked for hero ("JANE'S" over "PLANTS", leaf-J flanking), inline for nav. Never set it all-caps-bold-green — that's the generic plant-shop trap we're explicitly avoiding.
- **Tagline candidates:** "Grown indoors. Designed to thrive." / "Plants, properly." / "A glasshouse for your home."

### Positioning
Premium indoor-plant house for people who treat a plant like an object of design, not a impulse-buy at the garden centre. We sell **the plant, the vessel, the light, and the confidence to keep it alive.** indoorgrow's substance (grow kit, indoor focus, UK delivery) wearing Aesop/Apple-grade tailoring. Price-justified by curation, provenance ("grown in our Kent glasshouse"), and a care experience nobody else bothers to build.

### Personality
Knowledgeable but warm. Jane is a real grower, not a brand voice — she has opinions about drainage and she'll tell you which plant will die in your north-facing bathroom. Confident, a little witty, never twee. Think *botanist-meets-art-director*: precise Latin names AND "this one's basically unkillable."

### Three art directions

**A. Editorial Botanical** *(the safe-luxury one)*
Magazine-grade. Generous white/cream space, single hero specimens shot like fashion portraits, big serif headlines, lots of breathing room. Plants as sculpture against seamless paper backdrops. Pressed-botanical line illustrations as accents.
- *Moodboard:* Kinfolk + Aesop + a vintage botanical print catalogue. Cream paper, one perfect Monstera leaf raking shadow across it, brass caption rules, a hand holding secateurs. Quiet, expensive, restrained. Type does the talking.

**B. Lush Cinematic Jungle** *(the wow one — my lead recommendation)*
Dark, humid, backlit. Deep shadow with shafts of light cutting through layered foliage. Macro detail of water beads on a leaf, mist drifting, the green glowing from within. Everything feels filmed, not photographed. Heavy use of Gemini video loops — leaves swaying, droplets falling, fog rolling.
- *Moodboard:* A glasshouse at 6am, low sun through condensation. *Planet Earth* macro cinematography meets a high-end perfume ad. Near-black backgrounds, emerald and chartreuse light, brass and terracotta as the only warm notes. You should feel the humidity.

**C. Clean Futuristic Greenhouse / Bio-Tech** *(the differentiator)*
Bright, white, scientific-optimistic. Plants in a precision growing environment — clean lines, soft grow-light gradients (the magenta/cyan LED glow of real indoor horticulture), data-overlay aesthetics ("light: 14h · humidity: 65%"). Where the indoorgrow "growing" angle becomes the whole identity.
- *Moodboard:* SpaceX-clean meets a vertical farm. Frosted acrylic, soft pink-and-cyan LED bloom, floating UI cards with live plant vitals, a hand adjusting a dial. Optimistic, technical, "the future of growing indoors."

**→ Recommended:** Lead with **B (Lush Cinematic Jungle)** as the primary identity and borrow the data-overlay restraint of **C** for the care/tool surfaces. This gives emotional wow on the hero and storefront, and clean utility on the functional pages. A is the fallback if the client wants lower production risk.

### Typography pairings (all Google Fonts / Fontshare — free, production-safe)

**Pairing 1 — Editorial luxe (recommended):**
- **Display/Headlines:** *Fraunces* (Google) — a "soft serif" with optical sizing and a wonky-warm character at large sizes; carries the "Jane's" human side beautifully. Use the highest optical size + slight SOFT axis for headlines.
- **Body/UI:** *Satoshi* (Fontshare) — clean, modern, geometric-humanist grotesque; pairs cleanly under Fraunces.
- **Accent/labels:** *Fraunces* italic for botanical Latin names (*Monstera deliciosa*), small caps for nav.

**Pairing 2 — Cinematic/modern:**
- **Display:** *Editorial New* (Fontshare) — the iconic high-contrast modern serif of 2024-26 luxury web; razor-thin hairlines look incredible in cream on near-black.
- **Body:** *General Sans* (Fontshare) or *Inter* (Google).

**Pairing 3 — Bio-tech:**
- **Display:** *Clash Display* (Fontshare) — wide, confident, slightly technical.
- **Body/data:** *Space Grotesk* (Google) for the live-vitals UI numerals (light/humidity/temp), *Inter* for body.

**Standing rule:** never more than 2 type families on screen at once + 1 mono for data. Latin names always italic. Numerals (prices, vitals) get tabular/mono treatment so the "data" read stays clean.

## 3. SIGNATURE INTERACTIONS
*The strongest 6–8, all unique to a living-plant brand. Each is a reason to share the link.*

1. **Scroll-scrubbed growth time-lapse (the signature set-piece).** As you scroll the hero/story, a single seed germinates → sprouts → unfurls → becomes a full Monstera, scrubbed frame-by-frame to scroll position. Built from a Gemini-generated time-lapse video (or image sequence) driven by scroll. The plant literally grows as you read. This is *the* hero moment.

2. **Cursor that sprouts leaves.** A subtle trail of tiny chartreuse leaves/tendrils sprouts and curls from the cursor as it moves, then withers/fades after ~1s. On the product grid the cursor casts a soft "grow-light" glow that brightens the nearest plant. Performance-cheap (canvas/SVG), instantly memorable, on-brand.

3. **Day/Night greenhouse toggle.** A brass dial/sun-moon switch in the nav. **Day** = "Conservatory" cream palette, bright daylight photography. **Night** = "Bioluminescent" — lights dim, grow-LEDs bloom magenta/cyan, plants rim-light glow, ambient cricket/mist audio (opt-in), particle pollen drifts. Not a generic dark-mode toggle — a *time-of-day* in a glasshouse. The single most screenshot-worthy feature.

4. **Parallax layered foliage / depth corridors.** Hero and section transitions built from 4–6 separated foliage layers (foreground fronds, mid plants, background mist) moving at different scroll speeds, so you feel like you're walking *into* the jungle. Real depth, not a flat background.

5. **Interactive 3D plant inspector (on each product page).** Drag to rotate a real plant 360°, pinch to zoom into leaf veins, toggle hotspots ("new growth here", "where to cut", "root health"). Built from a Gemini-generated 360° turntable image sequence (cheaper/sharper than a true 3D model) with annotated hotspots. Lets people *inspect* before buying — solves the #1 online-plant anxiety.

6. **"Build Your Jungle" configurator.** Pick your room (light level: bright / medium / low-light bathroom), your vibe (sculptural / lush / minimal), your commitment level (unkillable → needy diva). The page composites a live preview of *your* plant grouping in a styled room, returns a shoppable bundle, and shows projected "fullness in 6 / 12 months." Gamified, personal, high-conversion — directly serves the indoorgrow "help me actually grow things" promise.

7. **Generative botanical particle systems.** Drifting pollen/spores in night mode; on the care pages, a "watering" interaction where droplets fall and the soil-moisture meter responds. Section dividers dissolve into floating seed particles that reassemble into the next section's plant. Ambient, alive, never static.

8. **Leaf-unfurl reveal on scroll-into-view.** Every section heading and product card reveals by *unfurling* — content masked behind an SVG leaf-shape that opens like a new frond, rather than a generic fade-up. A consistent motion signature so the whole site feels like it's growing as you move through it.

*Honourable mentions to hold in reserve:* mist-on-hover that fogs and clears the product image; a "tap to water" easter egg that makes a wilting plant perk up; scroll-velocity-driven leaf sway (faster scroll = more wind).

**Build/perf notes:** GSAP ScrollTrigger for scrub timelines; Lenis for smooth scroll; videos as AV1/WebM seamless loops with poster frames + `prefers-reduced-motion` fallbacks to stills; all heavy set-pieces lazy-loaded and disabled on low-power devices. Wow without wrecking Core Web Vitals.

## 4. SITE MAP & PAGE-BY-PAGE

```
/                 Home (the showpiece)
/story            Jane / the glasshouse / how we grow
/shop             Plant collection (the storefront)
/plant/[slug]     Individual plant page
/build            Build Your Jungle (configurator)
/care             Care guides hub + per-plant guides
/journal          Editorial / growing notes (SEO + brand depth)
/visit            Visit the glasshouse / contact
/cart, /checkout  Commerce
```

**Home — `/`**
Wow moment: full-bleed cinematic glasshouse hero with a Gemini video loop (mist + backlit fronds swaying). As you scroll, the **growth time-lapse** scrubs (seed → full plant) while the value props fade in beside it, and you parallax *into* the jungle. The day/night dial sits top-right, daring you to flip it. First 3 seconds must feel like a nature film, not a shop.

**Story — `/story`**
Wow moment: a horizontal "walk through the glasshouse" scroll — the camera tracks left-to-right past growing benches (Gemini video / wide pano), with Jane's narration unfurling in text. Provenance, the Kent glasshouse, the people. Editorial-botanical treatment, brass captions, one stunning portrait of Jane among the plants.

**Shop — `/shop`**
Wow moment: the **cursor grow-light** — each plant card sits in shadow until your cursor's glow falls on it, then it brightens and the leaves give a tiny sway. Filter by light-level / difficulty / size with the leaf-unfurl reveal on each card. Hover fogs the image with mist that clears to reveal the plant. Sticky filter rail styled as a brass plant-tag system.

**Individual plant — `/plant/[slug]`**
Wow moment: the **3D inspector** — drag to rotate the specimen 360°, zoom into leaf veins, tap hotspots for care/new-growth. Live "vitals" panel (light need / water / humidity / pet-safe / difficulty) in the bio-tech data style. "See it grow" mini time-lapse. Latin name in Fraunces italic, terracotta "Add to your jungle" CTA. Cross-sell: "pairs well with…" styled as companion planting.

**Build Your Jungle — `/build`**
Wow moment: the configurator composites your chosen plants into a styled room render in real time and projects 6/12-month fullness. Outputs a shoppable bundle + a personalised care calendar. The most interactive, sticky page on the site.

**Care guides — `/care`**
Wow moment: the **interactive watering / light simulator** — drag a slider from "too little" to "too much" light/water and watch the plant illustration respond (thriving → yellowing → wilting). Per-plant guides with the same vitals system. Clean bio-tech layout; this is where Direction C earns its keep.

**Journal — `/journal`**
Editorial growing notes, seasonal guides, "what's flowering now." SEO engine + brand depth. Editorial-botanical treatment, big imagery, slow reading.

**Visit / Contact — `/visit`**
Wow moment: night-mode by default — the glasshouse glowing in the dark, bio-lume particles drifting, a soft ambient loop. Map to the Kent glasshouse, opening times, "book a visit," newsletter ("Notes from the Glasshouse"). Calm, atmospheric close.
